PM2.5 Trend Analysis & Forecasting – Fresno County, CA

R

Project Overview: Analyzed long-term PM2.5 air pollution trends in Fresno County (2016–2025) using EPA Air Quality System data. Built a complete time-series workflow including seasonal decomposition, stationarity testing, rolling averages, and ARIMA forecasting.

Problem: Fresno County consistently ranks among the most polluted regions in the U.S. Understanding seasonal patterns and forecasting future pollution levels is critical for environmental policy and public health planning.

Data & Methods: Collected daily EPA PM2.5 data (Parameter Code 88101) and filtered for Fresno County. Aggregated to monthly averages, filled missing months, and constructed a properly indexed time series. Applied STL decomposition to isolate trend and seasonality, conducted Augmented Dickey-Fuller testing for stationarity, and built an ARIMA model to forecast 12 months ahead.

Outcome: Identified seasonal winter pollution spikes and long-term trend behavior. Produced a 12-month forecast with confidence intervals and compared annual averages against the EPA standard (12 µg/m³). Delivered a reproducible, portfolio-ready environmental time-series analysis project.
